Tonkatsu

Tonkatsu is a crispy and fried pork cutlet dish. A popular Japanese comfort food.

Ingredients

  • Pork loin or tenderloin
  • Salt and pepper
  • Flour
  • Eggs
  • Panko breadcrumbs
  • Oil
  • Sauce

Optional:

  • Short-grain rice
  • Japanese curry roux
  • Onion, carrots, potatoes

Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pork

  1. Trim excess fat (optional). If using pork loin, you can leave a thin layer of fat for extra flavor or trim it off for a cleaner bite.
  2. Pound the meat (if needed). If your pork is thick (over ¾ inch), lightly pound it with a meat mallet or rolling pin to an even thickness. This helps it cook evenly.
  3. Season both sides with salt and pepper. Don't overdo it—just enough to enhance the natural flavors.

Step 2: Bread the Cutlet

Tonkatsu follows a three-step breading process, which is key to achieving a crisp, airy crust.

  1. Dredge the pork in flour. Lightly coat the entire surface. Shake off excess.
  2. Dip it in beaten egg. Ensure even coverage—this helps the breadcrumbs adhere.
  3. Press into panko breadcrumbs. Use your hands to gently press the breadcrumbs in, ensuring a solid, even layer. Don't overpack—too much pressure can make the coating dense rather than crisp.

Step 3: Fry to Perfection

  1. Heat the oil to 340–350°F (170–175°C) in a deep pan or heavy-bottomed skillet. The oil should be deep enough to submerge at least half of the cutlet.
  2. Test the oil by dropping in a few panko crumbs. If they sizzle and rise immediately, it's ready.
  3. Fry the pork in batches. Carefully lower the cutlet into the oil. Cook for 3–4 minutes per side, flipping once, until deeply golden brown.
  4. Drain and rest. Transfer to a wire rack or paper towels. Letting it sit for 2–3 minutes helps the crust set.

Step 4: Slice and Serve

  1. Slice the tonkatsu into even strips, about ¾ inch wide. A sharp knife is crucial here—pressing too hard will crush the crust.
  2. Drizzle with tonkatsu sauce or serve it on the side for dipping.
  3. Pair with shredded cabbage (a classic accompaniment that balances the richness) or opt for rice and curry.

Optional: Make Japanese Curry with Rice

    If you want a heartier meal, adding Japanese curry transforms tonkatsu into katsu curry, a staple dish in Japan.

  1. Cook the rice according to package instructions. Short-grain Japanese rice is best.
  2. Sauté onions, carrots, and potatoes in a pan with a little oil.
  3. Add water or broth and bring to a simmer.
  4. Stir in Japanese curry roux. Let it dissolve and thicken.
  5. Serve with the tonkatsu. Place a portion of rice on a plate, ladle the curry next to it, and lay the crispy pork cutlet on top.
